Garden Room

Asked to design and obtain planning consent on a Grade II listed building in the Lake District National Park on a site where excavations were over two meters below the foundations of adjoining properties; my thoughts were which is the biggest challenge?

The planning officer was happy to discuss our proposals and through detailed consultation a scheme was agreed and planning consent was obtained.

With our structural engineer, we quickly agreed a method for the retaining walls and works quickly got underway.

The resulting sun room nestles into its surroundings perfectly.

 

                We required an extension to the rear of our cottage and contacted  Peter Temple with our ideas looking for help and advice.

The property is a Grade II listed building in the Lake District National Park. It is a difficult site, situated in the village, directly on the roadside with restricted access to the rear and set into rising ground. Peter handled all of the preliminary discussions with the planners to find acceptable solutions to any difficulties they foresaw, before submitting the planning application.
